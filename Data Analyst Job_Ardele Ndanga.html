Data Analyst Job Postings Analysis

Report by: Ardele Ndang
Date: 31 October 2025

Introduction

This analysis examines 400 data analyst job postings to identify key requirements that hiring companies advertise, with particular focus on minimum years of experience and required programming languages. The overarching aim is to determine the relationship between experience requirements, programming language skills, and salary expectations in the data analyst job market.

Years of Experience Analysis

Interpretation: The scatter plot reveals a surprisingly weak relationship between minimum years of experience and median salary estimates (correlation coefficient: 0.068). The trend line shows a positive (upward) direction with a slope of approximately $757 per additional year of experience required. However, the wide 95% confidence interval ribbon (shown in grey) indicates substantial uncertainty in this relationship.

This suggests that years of experience alone is not a strong predictor of salary in data analyst positions. The data points are widely dispersed across all experience levels, with salaries ranging from approximately $33,500 to $150,000. Notably, entry-level positions (0 years) show salary ranges comparable to positions requiring 5-10 years of experience, indicating that other factors such as technical skills, industry, location, and company size may play more significant roles in determining compensation.

Programming Language Analysis

Summary Statistics (Median with Interquartile Range):

  • Both (Python & R): Median = $69,000 (IQR: $59,375 - $88,500) | n=68
  • Neither: Median = $68,000 (IQR: $56,000 - $79,500) | n=254
  • Python: Median = $69,000 (IQR: $58,750 - $80,125) | n=60
  • R: Median = $61,500 (IQR: $56,250 - $72,000) | n=15

Interpretation: The box plot analysis reveals interesting patterns in salary distributions across different programming language requirements. Positions requiring "Both" Python and R command the highest median salary at $69,000 (IQR: $59,375 - $88,500), suggesting that versatility in programming languages is valued by employers.

Python-only positions show a similar median salary of $69,000 (IQR: $58,750 - $80,125), indicating strong market demand for Python skills. Positions with "Neither" specific programming language requirement show a median of $68,000 (IQR: $56,000 - $79,500), representing the largest category with 254 postings. R-only positions show a slightly lower median salary of $61,500 (IQR: $56,250 - $72,000).

Notable Outliers:

  • Both category: Four positions at $138,500-$150,000, suggesting some senior or specialized roles requiring both languages command premium salaries.
  • Python category: Six positions ranging from $113,000-$150,000, indicating high-value Python-focused roles, possibly in data science or machine learning domains.

The relatively similar salary ranges across categories suggest that while programming skills are important, they may not be the primary driver of salary differences. The presence of 254 positions requiring neither Python nor R indicates that many data analyst roles may focus on other tools such as SQL, Excel, or business intelligence platforms.
