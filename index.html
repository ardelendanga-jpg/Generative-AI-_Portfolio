About Me

I’m an early-career epidemiologist and researcher passionate about integrating technology and data science into public health research. With expertise in epidemiological analysis, research coordination, and AI-driven methods, I focus on advancing health outcomes in Sub-Saharan Africa—particularly among people living with HIV and related comorbidities.

Currently pursuing a PhD in Epidemiology and Health Science at the University of Bern, my work explores vascular health and hypertension. I aim to bridge the gap between scientific inquiry and real-world application through innovative tools like generative AI.

About the Course

The Generative AI for Work & Research course equips professionals and scholars with practical skills to apply AI tools across diverse domains. Through hands-on exercises and real-world examples, it teaches how to automate research workflows, enhance creativity, and use generative models responsibly.

This course encourages experimentation—combining theory, practice, and ethics—to help participants build smart systems that streamline productivity and research efficiency. My weekly submissions document this learning process.
